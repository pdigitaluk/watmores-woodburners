Why Chimney Sweeping Matters

A blocked or dirty chimney is more than an inconvenience — it's a serious safety risk. Creosote and soot deposits build up inside your flue every time you burn wood or coal, and if left unchecked they can ignite, causing a dangerous chimney fire that can spread rapidly through your home.

Beyond fire risk, a partially blocked chimney can allow carbon monoxide — a colourless, odourless, deadly gas — to leak back into your living space. Carbon monoxide poisoning claims lives every year in the UK, and many of these tragedies are entirely preventable with regular chimney maintenance.

Your home insurer may also require evidence of annual chimney sweeping. Failing to maintain your chimney could invalidate a claim arising from a chimney fire or related incident.

How Often Should You Sweep Your Chimney?

The frequency depends on your fuel type and how often you use your stove or fire.

Wood Burning Stoves

At least once a year for regular use. If you burn wood frequently through the winter — particularly in Norfolk's colder months — twice a year is recommended for optimal safety and efficiency.

Multi-Fuel & Coal

Coal and smokeless fuels produce more sulphurous deposits than wood. We recommend sweeping at least twice a year if you regularly burn these fuels in your Norwich or Norfolk home.

Open Fires

Open fires should be swept at least once a year — or twice if used frequently. The larger opening means more debris can fall in, and draw issues are more common.

Occasional Use / Before Season

Even if your stove is rarely used, we recommend an annual sweep before the burning season begins to clear any blockages, bird nests or debris that may have gathered over summer.

Chimney Sweeping FAQs

How long does a chimney sweep take in Norwich or Norfolk?

A standard chimney sweep typically takes between 45 minutes and 1.5 hours, depending on the type of appliance, the length of the flue and the amount of soot or debris present. We'll always work efficiently to minimise disruption to your day.

Do I need to do anything to prepare for the sweep?

Please ensure there's no lit fire in the grate on the day of your appointment and ideally allow the appliance to cool for several hours beforehand. Clear a small space around the fireplace — we'll bring dust sheets and take care of everything else.

Will the sweep make a mess in my home?

We use professional dust sheets and a powerful vacuum system throughout the sweep, so the risk of mess is minimal. Our team take pride in leaving your home exactly as we found it — or cleaner.

Do I need a chimney sweeping certificate for my insurance?

Many home insurers in the UK require evidence of annual chimney sweeping as a condition of covering chimney fire damage. Our certificated sweeps issue a chimney sweep certificate on completion, which you can provide to your insurer. We'd always recommend checking your policy.

Can you sweep a chimney for an open fire as well as a wood burner?

Yes — we sweep chimneys for open fires, wood burning stoves, multi-fuel stoves, oil boilers and Aga-type appliances across Norwich and Norfolk. Whatever your setup, our team have the equipment and expertise to get the job done safely and thoroughly.

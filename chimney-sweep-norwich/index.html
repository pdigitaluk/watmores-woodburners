Chimney Sweeping in Norwich

Watmore's Woodburners are based in Norwich — NR7 — so we know the city's housing stock well. Norwich has one of the highest concentrations of Victorian and Edwardian terraced housing in the East of England, and a huge number of these properties retain their original chimney flues, many of which are in daily use with a wood burning stove or open fireplace.

We cover every area of the city — from the older terraces in NR1, NR2 and NR3 to the interwar semis in Eaton and Catton, the riverside properties in Thorpe St Andrew, and the newer estates on the city fringe. Annual sweeping is required by most UK home insurance policies and we issue a sweep certificate on every visit.

Local property types

Properties We Sweep in Norwich

Victorian and Edwardian terraces — NR1 to NR3

The inner city areas of Norwich have a dense spread of Victorian and Edwardian terraced houses. Many still have their original brick flues and open fireplaces, or have been fitted with stove liners in more recent years. We sweep these regularly and know the common flue configurations well.

Interwar semis in Eaton, Catton and Hellesdon

The interwar suburban belt around Norwich — Eaton, Old Catton, Hellesdon, Costessey — has thousands of semi-detached properties with period chimney stacks. These are among the most common jobs we carry out and are typically straightforward annual sweeps.

Thorpe St Andrew riverside and city-fringe properties

Thorpe St Andrew has a mix of older riverside properties and newer detached houses, many of which have had wood burning stoves fitted. We also cover Sprowston, Drayton, Taverham and all other city-fringe villages on our regular Norwich rounds.

Common questions

Norwich Chimney Sweep FAQs

How often should I have my chimney swept in Norwich?

At least once a year for wood burners and solid fuel appliances. Most UK home insurers require this annually. We issue a sweep certificate after every visit.

Do you cover all parts of Norwich?

Yes — we cover the whole of Norwich including Eaton, Thorpe St Andrew, Old Catton, Hellesdon, Lakenham, Mile Cross, Sprowston, Costessey, Taverham and Drayton. We're based in NR7 so response times are fast.

Do I need a sweep certificate for my home insurance in Norwich?

Most UK home insurers require evidence of annual chimney sweeping for properties with solid fuel appliances. We issue an official certificate on the day of every visit.

How long does a chimney sweep take in Norwich?

Around 45–60 minutes. We use dust sheets throughout and leave your home completely clean and tidy.

Can you sweep both a stove liner and an open fireplace in Norwich?

Yes — we sweep all flue types including stainless steel flexible liners, clay flue pots and original brick chimney flues. All common in Norwich's varied housing stock.

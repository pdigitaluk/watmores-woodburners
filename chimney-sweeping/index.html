How often

Sweeping Frequency Guide

How often you should have your chimney swept depends on how much you use it and what you burn.

Wood

Wood Burning Stoves

Sweep at least once per year — ideally at the end of winter before the stove is put to rest. Heavy users should sweep twice yearly.

Minimum: Once per year
Coal

Solid Fuel / Coal

Coal and smokeless fuels produce more deposits than wood. We recommend sweeping twice per year if you burn coal regularly.

Recommended: Twice per year
Gas

Gas Fires

Gas fires should be swept annually too. While they produce fewer deposits, they can still develop blockages from debris and bird nests.

Minimum: Once per year

Common questions

Chimney Sweeping FAQs

Everything you need to know about chimney sweeping in Norwich and Norfolk.

How often should I have my chimney swept in Norfolk?

For wood burning stoves, we recommend at least once per year — ideally at the end of the burning season. For solid fuel and coal, twice per year is advised. Most home insurance policies require annual sweeping as a condition of cover.

Do I get a certificate after a chimney sweep?

Yes — we issue an insurance-valid sweep certificate on every visit. This is essential for your home insurance and will be needed if you ever make a fire-related claim.

How long does a chimney sweep take?

A standard sweep takes approximately 45–60 minutes. We always work cleanly and efficiently to minimise disruption to your day.

Will the sweep make a mess in my home?

No — we use dust sheets, specialist equipment and a powerful vacuum to ensure your home stays clean. Most customers are surprised at how tidy we leave things.

Wood Burning Stoves in Norwich

Watmore's Woodburners are based in Norwich — NR7 — and carry out more stove installations within the city than anywhere else in our service area. Norwich has an exceptionally varied housing stock: from Victorian and Edwardian terraces in the inner city with original chimney flues, to interwar semis across Eaton, Catton and Hellesdon, to modern detached houses on the city fringe where a twin-wall flue system makes a stove installation possible without an existing chimney.

Local property types

Installations We Carry Out in Norwich

Victorian and Edwardian terraces — NR1 to NR3

Norwich has one of the densest concentrations of Victorian terraced housing in the East of England. These properties typically have original brick chimney flues running through the stack — well-suited to a stainless steel liner and a 5kW stove. We install in these regularly and know the common flue layouts and any quirks to look out for.

Interwar semis in Eaton, Catton and Hellesdon

The interwar suburban belt around Norwich — Eaton, Old Catton, Hellesdon, Costessey — has thousands of semi-detached properties, many with fireplaces that were blocked off in the 1970s and 80s. Opening these up and installing a modern stove is one of the most satisfying jobs we do. We confirm flue condition before quoting.

Modern houses and extensions on the city fringe

Newer properties in Sprowston, Drayton, Taverham, Poringland and other city-fringe villages often don't have a chimney at all. We solve this with a twin-wall flue system routed through the ceiling or externally — a fully compliant installation at a fixed price, included in our standard packages.

Wood Burning Stove FAQs

Do I need planning permission to install a wood burning stove in Norfolk?

In most cases you don't need planning permission for a standard wood burning stove installation. However, if you live in a listed building or conservation area in Norwich or Norfolk, additional requirements may apply. Our team will advise you during your free survey.

What size stove do I need for my room?

As a general guide, allow approximately 1kW of heat output per 14 cubic metres of room volume. Our engineers will calculate the exact output required for your room during the free home survey to ensure efficient, comfortable heating.

Can I burn any wood in my new stove?

For best results and to comply with the Clean Air Act, we recommend burning dry, well-seasoned or Woodsure Ready to Burn certified logs with a moisture content below 20%. Burning wet or unseasoned wood produces excess creosote and reduces efficiency significantly.

How long does a stove installation take?

A typical wood burning stove installation in Norwich or Norfolk takes between one and two days, depending on the complexity of the work involved. We'll give you a clear timeline when we provide your quote.

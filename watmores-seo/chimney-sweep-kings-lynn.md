## H2: King's Lynn Housing — What We See Most

**Period properties in the town centre:** The historic core of King's Lynn has a remarkable number of original chimney breasts, many with Victorian insert fireplaces or older open hearths. These often have multiple pots on a single stack serving different rooms. We sweep each flue separately and confirm which appliance each one serves.

Many of these were converted to gas decades ago and then back to open fires or wood burners more recently. These transitional flues often need particularly thorough cleaning.

## H2: King's Lynn Chimney Sweep FAQs

**I have a period property in the King's Lynn Conservation Area — can you sweep listed building chimneys?**
Yes. We work in Grade II and Grade II* listed properties regularly. The sweeping process is the same, but we take extra care with original masonry and will flag anything that may need a specialist mason's attention. We do not use rotary power tools on original clay liner systems.

**How does the proximity to The Wash affect how often I need my chimney swept?**
Coastal and estuarine air carries more moisture, which combines with combustion gases in the flue to accelerate acidic tar deposits — particularly in appliances that aren't burned hot enough. We recommend west Norfolk coastal properties sweep annually as a minimum, even with light usage. If you're burning wood that isn't fully seasoned (moisture content above 20%), sweep every six months.

**Do I need a sweep certificate to satisfy my home insurer?**
Yes — most UK home insurance policies for solid fuel properties require an annual sweep by a registered professional, with a certificate as evidence. Our HETAS certificates are accepted by all major insurers.

**How quickly can you get to King's Lynn?**
We typically have availability within the week. King's Lynn is a regular part of our west Norfolk run and we're usually in the area at least once a week.

**Can you sweep an open fireplace I've never used since buying the house?**
Yes — and we'd strongly recommend it before lighting anything. We'll inspect the flue, confirm it's clear of blockages, nesting, and debris, and issue a certificate once it's safe to use.

## H2: Considerations for Mid-Norfolk Properties

**Barn conversions and agricultural buildings:** Mid-Norfolk has a significant number of converted barns and agricultural buildings that have been turned into residential properties over the past 30 years. These present different installation challenges to a conventional terrace — the ceiling heights are often greater, the walls thicker, and the flue route less obvious. We survey these carefully, work through the options, and recommend the most practical approach. In many cases a twin-wall flue system through the roof is the right answer; in others, an existing original chimney stack can be lined.

**Off-grid farmhouses:** Properties that rely entirely on oil, LPG, or biomass heating are among the best candidates for wood burner installation. The economics are straightforward — a well-run wood burner using seasoned hardwood bought locally costs significantly less per kWh than oil or LPG in most winters. We install a high proportion of our systems in off-grid rural properties across mid-Norfolk.

**Victorian terraces in Dereham town:** The residential streets around the town centre have the familiar pattern of Victorian workers' housing: chimney breast, original fireplace opening, sound original flue. Where the flue is in good condition, these installations are efficient and cost-effective. Where the original clay liner has deteriorated — common in properties that haven't had an open fire in 30+ years — we fit a new stainless steel flexible liner as part of the installation.

**Properties with Aga or Rayburn range cookers:** A number of Dereham area farmhouses have oil-fired Aga or Rayburn range cookers that use the same chimney stack as the living room flue. We're experienced in working in these properties and can install a wood burner using a separate liner in the same stack, or route the stove flue through a different stack if the layout allows. We do a thorough survey before advising.

## H2: Dereham Woodburner FAQs

**My mid-Norfolk property is off the gas grid — is a woodburner a good primary heat source?**
Yes, in the right property. A 5kW stove will comfortably heat a living space of up to 60–70 cubic metres. For an open-plan kitchen-diner or a larger living room, we'd recommend a higher-output stove — we advise on this during the survey, based on the room dimensions and insulation levels. Many off-grid mid-Norfolk households run a wood burner as primary heat in the main living area and use oil or electric as backup in bedrooms and bathrooms.

**I have a barn conversion near Dereham — can you install a woodburner in it?**
Yes, we work in converted agricultural buildings regularly. The survey takes a little longer in these properties as the flue route needs more careful planning, but there's almost always a workable solution. Twin-wall flue through the roof is often the most practical answer in a barn conversion with no existing chimney.

**Does the installation satisfy building regulations?**
Yes. Every installation we carry out is HETAS registered, which is a competent person scheme approved by the government. This means we self-certify the installation to Building Regulations on your behalf — you don't need to apply for building regs approval separately, and the HETAS certificate we issue is the official notification. This is important for future property sales, insurance, and mortgage purposes.

**How far in advance should I book?**
We're busiest from September through November as people want stoves installed before winter. If you're planning an installation for the heating season, we'd recommend getting in touch in August or September. Winter and spring enquiries typically have shorter lead times.

**Can you install a larger stove for a bigger room?**
Yes — our three standard packages use 5kW appliances which suit the majority of residential rooms. For larger rooms, open-plan layouts, or properties using the stove as a primary heat source, we can supply and install higher-output appliances. We advise on the right output during the survey.

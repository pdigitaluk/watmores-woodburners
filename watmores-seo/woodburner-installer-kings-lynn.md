## H2: Considerations for King's Lynn Properties

**Listed buildings:** Many of King's Lynn's oldest properties — the Hanseatic Warehouses, the merchants' houses on King Street, and the various Grade II buildings throughout the Conservation Area — are listed. Installing a wood burner in a listed property is entirely possible and, in most cases, does not require listed building consent if the flue runs inside the existing chimney stack. We advise on this as part of every survey and can confirm whether consent is needed before any work is quoted.

**Properties with existing but unused fireplaces:** King's Lynn has a large number of properties — particularly the Victorian terraces in residential areas — where the original fireplace opening is still intact behind a board or a gas fire surround. These are the simplest and most cost-effective woodburner installations: the opening is already there, the chimney already exists, it just needs a flue liner and a stove fitted. We see these every week across the town.

**New flue installations:** Some King's Lynn properties — particularly 20th century housing where the original fireplace was removed entirely — need a twin-wall flue system installed, either internally up through the property or externally up the outside wall. We design and install both types. External twin-wall installations are often the most practical option for semi-detached and terraced properties where routing through the property is difficult.

**Off-gas villages in west Norfolk:** The villages surrounding King's Lynn — Castle Rising, Gayton, Grimston, Congham — are largely off the mains gas network. For these properties, a wood burner can be the most cost-effective primary or secondary heating solution available. We do a high proportion of our installations in off-grid rural properties.

## H2: King's Lynn Woodburner FAQs

**Do I need planning permission to install a woodburner in my King's Lynn home?**
Most standard residential installations in King's Lynn don't require planning permission. The exception is if your property is listed, in a Smoke Control Area, or if you're making external changes to the building. King's Lynn is not a Smoke Control Area, so you can burn seasoned wood freely. We handle all Building Regulations notification as part of every installation.

**Can you install a woodburner in a listed property in the King's Lynn Conservation Area?**
Yes, we work in listed properties regularly. In most cases, installing a stove inside an existing fireplace opening with a flue liner running inside the original chimney stack does not require listed building consent, as no structural changes are made. We advise on each case individually during the survey.

**How long does installation take?**
The majority of our installations complete in a single day. The survey visit takes 30–45 minutes. Installation day runs from morning to afternoon. You're able to use the stove that evening.

**My house in King's Lynn doesn't have a chimney — can you still install a woodburner?**
Yes. We install twin-wall flue systems for properties without an existing chimney. The flue runs either through the room above and out through the roof, or up the outside of the property. We'll advise on the best route during the survey visit.

**What's the best stove size for a typical King's Lynn terrace?**
For a standard living room in a 2–3 bedroom terraced house, a 5kW stove is usually the right size — enough to heat the room comfortably without being so powerful that you can't run it at a sensible burn rate. Our three standard packages all use 5kW appliances for this reason.

## H2: Mid-Norfolk Housing — What We See Most

**Farmhouses and agricultural conversions:** The countryside between Dereham and Swaffham, and out towards the Brecks, has a large number of older farmhouses and converted outbuildings. These often have substantial chimney systems — sometimes original Tudor or Jacobean stacks — that have been modified, extended, or partially blocked over the centuries. We approach these carefully, confirming which flue serves which appliance before sweeping begins.

**Aga and Rayburn range cookers:** A much higher proportion of mid-Norfolk properties use oil-fired or solid fuel range cookers compared to Norwich or the coast. These require annual flue sweeping just like an open fireplace — the flue accumulates soot and sooty deposits from the range's constant low-level burn. We sweep Aga and Rayburn flues as part of our regular service across the Dereham area.

**Off-grid properties:** Many villages in the Dereham area — particularly the smaller settlements of 50–200 houses — have no mains gas connection at all. For these properties, solid fuel heating is not supplementary but primary. We see a lot of wood burners being used as the main heat source in these homes, which means higher annual usage and more frequent sweeping requirements.

**Victorian market town properties:** The centre of Dereham has a familiar mid-Norfolk mix of Victorian terraced housing and former agricultural workers' cottages. These typically have the original chimney breast and flue still in place, often with an open fireplace or an early insert fire. Many are now being converted to wood burners as owners look to reduce oil and electric costs.

## H2: Dereham Chimney Sweep FAQs

**Can you sweep an Aga or Rayburn flue as well as my wood burner?**
Yes — we sweep all solid fuel appliance flues, including oil-fired Agas and Rayburns, wood-burning range cookers, and open fires. If you have multiple solid fuel appliances in the same property, we can sweep them all in a single visit. We issue a separate certificate for each flue swept.

**My cottage is in a small village outside Dereham — do you cover it?**
Almost certainly yes. We cover a wide area of mid-Norfolk from Dereham outwards. If you're not sure whether you're in our area, just call or WhatsApp and we'll confirm. We're out this way regularly.

**I've bought a farmhouse where nobody has lived for a few years — should I get the chimney swept before lighting the fire?**
Absolutely, and we'd strongly recommend it before anything is lit. Unoccupied properties accumulate bird nests, debris, and in some cases partial blockages that can be invisible from below. We'll do a full inspection sweep, confirm everything is clear, and issue a certificate once it's safe to use.

**How often do I need to sweep if I use my wood burner as a main heat source?**
If you're burning through wood all winter as your primary heating, we'd recommend sweeping every six months — once in early autumn before the heating season starts and once in spring after it ends. Annual sweeping is the minimum for insurance, but heavy users will accumulate deposits faster than a once-a-year sweep can keep pace with.

**Does the sweep certificate satisfy my home insurance requirement?**
Yes. Our HETAS certificates are accepted by all major UK home insurers and confirm the date, the appliance swept, and our registered credentials. Most solid fuel insurance policies require annual sweeping by a registered professional as a condition of cover.
